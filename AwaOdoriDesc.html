Description

All of the spectators excited to come to Tokushima to see the "Foll's Dance" whose origins date back 400 years. They come on between August 12th and August 15. The "Fool's Dance" comes from the lyrics to common dance song, which translate as : "Fools dance and fools watch, if both are fools, you might as well dance".


Although some events happen during the daytime, the main spectacle takes place between 18:00 and 22:30, when groups of dancers called "ren" perform in blocked off streets of downtown Tokushima. The dancers come from Tokushima, and other parts of Japan and as far away as the United States.


Tokushima's city center close in the evening and turned into a large dance stage. There are six different type of stage areas with either free seating or paid seating. People who pay for the seating will see clearer view than people who did not pay to view ethe event. There will be many food and game stalls outstide the stage areas for the visitors to fill their stomach and enjoy during the events.


Visitors who wants to see Awa Odori will face difficulty to find places to stay because the hotels will fully booked a month before the event start. Many spectators have to stay elsewhere; options include nearby Naruto (40 minutes by train), Takamatsu (1 hour by train), or even Osaka (2.5 hours by car).


For guests to Tokushima who miss the celebration, an exhibition hall about the Awa Odori is situated in the Awa Odori Kaikan. There you will also find a dance hall where dance demonstrations are held several times a day. What's more, the lower station of the ropeway paving the way to Mount Bizan is situated in the Awa Odori Kaikan building.

Price

Tickets for the four reserved stage areas can be purchased in advance or on the day. The tickets price will range between 900 yen to 2200 yen for two performances. First perfomance will be on 18.00 to 20.00 while the second performance from 20.30 to 22.30. The tickets will be available at convenience stores nationwide. On the same, you can get the ticket at the tourist information center in front of Tokushima Station and outside of the reserved stage on festival nights.
